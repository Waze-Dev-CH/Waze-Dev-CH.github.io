# First steps for a new editor

Welcome! You have just started editing the Waze map in Switzerland, or you are about to. This page brings together the essentials to get off to a good start: understanding your level, opening the editor, installing the right tool, and knowing what you can safely edit.

::: note You are a beginner — going slow is normal
Level 1 (L1) is the discovery level. Nobody expects perfect edits from you on day one. What matters: make small, safe edits, read before you act, and ask when in doubt.
:::

## Understanding level L1

Every new editor starts at level 1. This level defines what you can edit and where.

| | Level 1 |
| --- | --- |
| Editing radius | 1.6 km around your trips |
| Minimum edits | 0 (automatic attribution) |
| Locking | you can edit segments locked at level 1 |
| Duration | driving-based rights expire after 3 months of inactivity |

Promotion to level 2 is automatic once the criteria are met. For the full details on levels and roles, see [Community organisation](/en/editors/organisation-communaute).

## Opening the editor (WME)

Edits are made in the **Waze Map Editor (WME)**, available at [waze.com/editor](https://www.waze.com/editor) with your Waze account.

Take the time to get familiar with the interface before changing anything. Your editing area is located around the trips you make with the app: start with a place you know well, such as your neighbourhood.

## Installing the right tool

::: help Tip — WME Switzerland Helper
Install the **WME Switzerland Helper** userscript right from the start. It adds Swiss geographic layers, public transport data (SBB stops) and a street-name check — all valuable help for editing according to local rules.
:::

The script is installed through a userscript manager such as Tampermonkey. Details and other tools are on the [Resources](/en/editors/ressources) page.

## The trap to avoid at all costs

::: important Important — Never apply a neighbouring country's rules
Switzerland has its own editing rules. A practice that is valid in France, Germany or Italy is **not necessarily** valid here, even though we share a border and a language. Never apply a rule by simple analogy.
:::

The right reflex: read the Wiki and the forum before editing, and ask when in doubt.

::: success Success — One more question is better
One more question is always better than an edit that has to be corrected.
:::

## What can I safely edit at L1?

For your first edits, stick to simple, low-risk changes:

- correcting a street name according to Swiss conventions (see [Naming roads and cities](/en/editors/nommage-routes-villes));
- slightly adjusting the geometry of a segment you know on the ground;
- handling simple reports.

::: failure Error — Keep this for later
At the start, avoid topics that require experience and often a higher level: complex intersections, [roundabouts](/en/editors/ronds-points), [lanes](/en/editors/lanes) (L3+) or [permanent hazards](/en/editors/dangers-permanents) (L4+). Watch how experienced editors work, and ask before diving in.
:::

## First-steps checklist

1. Have an active Waze account (use the app on your trips).
2. Open the [Waze Map Editor](https://www.waze.com/editor).
3. Install the **WME Switzerland Helper** userscript (see [Resources](/en/editors/ressources)).
4. Read the basics: [Naming roads and cities](/en/editors/nommage-routes-villes), [Segment types and routing](/en/editors/types-segments-routing) and the [Swiss map legend](/en/editors/legende-carte).
5. Join the Romandie forum and the community Discord (see below).
6. Make a first small, simple edit, in an area you know.

## Where to find help

Before asking a question, check the Wiki and the forum: the answer is often already there. Otherwise:

- **Romandie forum**: for editing questions in French-speaking Switzerland (pinned topics) — https://www.waze.com/forum/viewforum.php?f=836
- **Community Discord**: for more direct exchanges — https://discord.gg/dmxUwvTkk5
