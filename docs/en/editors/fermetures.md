# Road closures

This page covers the management of road closures in Switzerland: temporary closures (Real Time Closures and Major Traffic Events) and the handling of reports of construction sites or closures.

## Temporary closures

Temporary closures are used to represent in Waze a road that is inaccessible for a set period of time: construction site, event, emergency works, etc.

### Real Time Closures (RTC)

Real Time Closures are closures applied directly to a segment, with a start and end date. They block routing on the affected segment during the defined period.

::: note Procedural details to be completed
The detailed procedure for creating RTCs (interface, required permissions, handling of traffic directions) is not reproduced in the sources consulted. Check the Swiss forum and the Discord server before applying a closure.
:::

### Major Traffic Events (MTE)

Major Traffic Events group several closures linked to the same event (event, race, large construction site). They allow a set of closures to be planned and activated in a coordinated way.

::: note Procedural details to be completed
The procedure for creating and activating MTEs, as well as the required permissions, are not detailed in the sources consulted.
:::

## Handling a report of a construction site or closure

Reports of construction sites and closures come up regularly on the Swiss forum. Examples observed: closures in Basel, Zurich, on the A3 and A9, works in Yvonand (VD) or Bellerivestrasse in Zurich.

General procedure:

- Verify the reality and the duration of the closure from a reliable source (official signage, cantonal announcement, photos).
- When in doubt, post the question on the Swiss forum rather than applying a change.
- Never rely on the rules of neighboring countries or on assumptions to edit the map in Switzerland.

::: important Caution rule
When in doubt, in case of missing information or an ambiguous situation, do not edit the map based on assumptions or on the rules of a neighboring country. Always ask the question on the Swiss forum.
:::
