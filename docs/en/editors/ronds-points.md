# Roundabouts

The intersections guide points to a dedicated guide for roundabouts. This page covers the available principles and the Swiss locking standard.

## General principle

A roundabout is handled like a full intersection: you look for simplicity first, so that the router produces the correct entry and exit instructions. The intersections guide places roundabouts in the "Between roads" section and points to a dedicated wiki page ("Everything about Roundabouts").

::: note Detailed content not reproduced
The detailed content of the dedicated roundabout guide (step-by-step construction, naming, special cases) is not accessible from the consulted sources. The precise drawing rules are therefore not reproduced here, so as not to invent them. Refer to the dedicated wiki guide indicated in the intersections guide.
:::

## Swiss standard: minimum L3 locking

::: important Swiss community rule
In Switzerland, the recommended minimum locking level for a roundabout is L3.
:::

This rule is applied by the Swiss community and checked by the WME Switzerland Helper script, which flags roundabouts whose locking level is below L3. Locking a roundabout at L3 protects a frequently edited geometry and prevents accidental changes by low-level editors.
