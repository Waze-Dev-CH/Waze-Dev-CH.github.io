# Swiss map legend

The map legend describes the segment types used in Switzerland and how they are displayed in the Waze editor (WME). Each road type has a specific use and consequences on routing.

## What the legend is for

The choice of segment type directly influences guidance. A wrong type can send drivers onto unsuitable roads or, conversely, exclude them from valid routes. The legend serves as a common reference so that all editors name and classify roads in the same way.

The type is chosen based on the official signage visible on the ground, not on the assumed importance of the road.

## Express roads (Fernstrassen)

- **Autobahn (Freeway)**: carriageways separated by direction, without lights or stops, accessible only via ramps. Green signs. In Switzerland, the motorway takes precedence over the national road where the two overlap.
- **Federal road, Bundesstrasse (Major Highway)**: semi-motorways and express roads not classified as Autobahn. Often a single lane, rarely separated by direction, generally grade-separated but different from Autobahn. Green signs.
- **National or main road, Landesstrasse (Minor Highway)**: all numbered main and national roads. The numbering follows the signage visible at the exits of localities.
- **Ramp**: motorway entrance and exit ramps only.

## Urban roads (Strassen)

- **Main street, Hauptstrasse (Primary Street)**: according to the signage.
- **Street, Strasse (Street)**: secondary streets, neighbourhood streets and standard roads. White signs. One-way or two-way.
- **Access road, Zufahrtsstrasse (Access Road)**: not used in Switzerland.
- **Parking, Parkplatz (Parking Lot Road)**: large parking lots, rest-area parking lots and parking lanes.
- **Private road, Privatstrasse (Private Road)**: excluded from general routing, marked as private.
- **Pedestrian path, Fussweg (Walking Trail)**: must never connect two routable roads to each other.
- **Pedestrian zone, Fussgängerzone (Pedestrian Boardwalk)**: pedestrian zones and promenades. Same non-connection rule as pedestrian paths.
- **Stairway, Treppe (Stairway)**: same connection restriction.
- **Railway, Eisenbahn (Railroad)**: requires a high editing level. No connection to a street.
- **Runway, Landebahn (Runway/Taxiway)**: exhaustive mapping is discouraged.

::: important Do not invent values
As long as a value is not confirmed by the Swiss Wiki or the forum, do not apply it on assumption. When in doubt, ask on the Romandie forum or on the community Discord.
:::
