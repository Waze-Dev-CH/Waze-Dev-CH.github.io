# Naming roads and cities in Switzerland

This page summarises the naming rules for streets, roads and cities when editing in Switzerland. The Swiss reference always takes precedence over the guides of neighbouring countries.

::: important The Swiss reference takes precedence
In case of doubt, missing information or a similar situation, never make a change on WME in Switzerland based on editing rules from neighbouring countries. Consult the Swiss community sources first.
:::

## Basic principles

Use the official, full name of the road, as it appears on signage. Capitalisation and accents are part of the official name and must be respected.

::: failure Abbreviations to avoid
Do not abbreviate the type of road. Write the name out in full:

- "Chemin" and not "Ch."
- "Route" and not "Rte"
- "Avenue" and not "Av."
:::

::: success Correct case and accents
- "Rue de la Paix" (and not "rue de la paix")
- Keep the accents: é, è, à, ç
:::

## Types of roads in Switzerland

The Swiss classification maps WME types to the real road categories:

| WME type | Swiss category |
| --- | --- |
| Autoroute (Freeway) | Motorway / Semi-motorway |
| Route majeure (Major Highway) | Semi-motorway |
| Route mineure (Minor Highway) | National and main road |
| Rue primaire (Primary Street) | Main road |
| Rue (Street) | All other streets, secondary streets, etc. |
| Rampe (Ramp) | Motorway entrances and exits |

## Motorways and semi-motorways

- Motorways: numbering A1, A2, etc.
- Semi-motorways: numbering A1L, A52, etc.

## National and main roads

National roads are numbered from N1 to N30.

- National road in town: street name, and add the number (N1, N2) as an alternative name via "Add a different name".
- National road outside town: only the number (N1, N2, etc.), without a street name.

For numbered main roads:

- In town: with the street name.
- Outside town: without the street name.

## Entrances and exits

Use the ">" sign to indicate the direction (spoken by the voice synthesis) and "/" to separate cities.

::: example Examples of exits and entrances
- "Sortie 10 > Zürich / Flughafen"
- "A2 > Basel"
:::

Junctions and interchanges receive a named area following the "17 Frick" model.

## Bilingual names

For a road bearing a name in two languages, enter the second name via the alternative name field ("Add a different name"). The primary name remains the road's main official name.

## Railways

Railways never bear a city name or a street name. Leave the name field empty.

## Cities and villages

Only the names of cities appearing in the official list of Swiss cities must be used.

## To adapt from the France guide

The French naming guide contains conventions that do not apply in Switzerland. Not to be transposed as is:

::: important French rules, not Swiss
The following items come from the France guide and do not apply in Switzerland:

- French road nomenclature (motorways A, national N, departmental D, communal roads C): Switzerland uses its own classifications (motorways A, national roads N1 to N30, etc.).
- The formats specific to French ring roads and beltways (for example "A86 - Intérieure").
- The European numbering format specific to the French network (for example "E72").
- The French format for motorway exits and entrances ("Sortie 18: Valensole", "A6a: Paris"): in Switzerland, the ">" and "/" signs are used (see above).

What remains valid in Switzerland from this guide: the principle of the official and full name based on signage, respecting capitalisation, accents and hyphens, and the ban on common abbreviations.
:::
