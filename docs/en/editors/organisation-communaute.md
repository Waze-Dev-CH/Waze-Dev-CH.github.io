# Community organisation

The Swiss Waze community is part of the DACH zone (Germany, Austria, Switzerland). It relies on supervisory roles and on editor levels. This page explains who does what and where to ask a question.

## Editor levels

An editor progresses through levels. The level determines the radius within which they can edit and the lock level of segments they can modify. Levels 1 and 2 are automatic. From level 3 onwards, the upgrade is requested by private message (PM) to the self-management team.

| Level | Editing radius | Minimum edits | Granting |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1.6 km | 0 | Automatic (gets familiar with the wiki and the forum) |
| 2 | 3.2 km | 3,000 | Automatic (has read the wiki, follows the rules) |
| 3 | 4.8 km | 25,000 | On request (PM) to the self-management team |
| 4 | 6.4 km | 100,000 | On request (PM) to the self-management team |
| 5 | 6.4 km | 250,000 | On request (PM) to the self-management team |
| 6 | 6.4 km | 500,000 | On request (PM) to the self-management team |

::: important Key rules
Driving-related rights expire after 3 months. A locked segment can only be edited by an editor of a level greater than or equal to the lock level. Failure to respond to private messages can lead to a downgrade or a suspension of the account.
:::

## Supervisory roles

Map management relies on several roles, in addition to the editor level.

- **Area Manager (AM)**: full editing within their territory, subject to locking rules according to their level.
- **State Manager (SM)**: full editing within the canton assigned to them, subject to the same locking rules.
- **RTC State Manager**: a role for managing road closures, specific to Switzerland (it exists only in this country). See [Road closures](/en/editors/fermetures).
- **Country Manager (CM)**: support at the national level, enforcement of the rules and community moderation.

::: note Up-to-date list
The list of names of CMs, SMs and AMs changes over time. Consult the dedicated section of the Swiss Wiki rather than relying on a frozen copy.
:::

## Where and when to ask a question

Before asking a question, consult the Wiki and the forum: the answer is often already there.

- **Romandie forum**: for editing questions in French-speaking Switzerland (see the pinned topics).
- **Community Discord**: for more direct exchanges.
- **Status Page**: to check ongoing events on the Waze side.
- **Email support**: as a last resort only.

::: important When in doubt
If you are unsure about a rule, ask first on the Romandie forum or Discord. Never apply a change based on the rules of a neighbouring country.
:::
